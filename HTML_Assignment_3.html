Stanford reseachers find a quicker, cheaper way to sort isotopes

BY EMMANUEL ROMERO L.A. Ciecero Richard Zare Professor Richard Zare in his lab. Whether it's the summer grass that tickles your feet or thea red Bordeaux smacking on your palate, nearly every part of the world around you carries special chemical markes. These markers, called isotopes, can tell scientists where the molecules that compose a substance are from, where they traveled and what happened to them along the way. But doing these analyses has been complex and costly. Now, Stanford chemists have developed a new methods to make isotopic analysis easier and less expensive.
